Scoring

Every winning hand in riichi mahjong is scored using two values: han and fu. Han come from the yaku (scoring patterns) in your hand and any dora. Fu come from the specific shape of your hand and how you won. Together, they determine how many points you win.

If your hand has 5 or more han, fu don't matter — the score is determined by han alone. Fu only matter for hands with 1–4 han.

Use the calculator at the bottom of this page to look up your score once you know your han and fu.

Han

Han are the primary unit of hand value. Each yaku is worth a set number of han, and a hand can combine multiple yaku. Dora tiles also add han, but they are not yaku on their own — your hand must have at least one yaku to win.

Some yaku are worth fewer han (or are unavailable entirely) when your hand is open — that is, when you've called chi, pon, or kan. A × in the "Open" column means the yaku cannot be used with an open hand.

Fu

Fu are base points determined by the shape of your hand and how you won. To calculate fu, add up the values from the five categories below, then round the total up to the nearest 10 (e.g. 32 fu becomes 40 fu).

① Base Fu

Every hand starts with 20 fu.

② Winning Method

MethodFu
Tsumo (self-draw)2
Ron with a closed hand10
Ron with an open hand0

③ Melds (Sets)

Each set in your hand contributes fu based on whether it's open or closed, and whether it uses simple tiles (2–8) or terminal/honor tiles (1, 9, winds, dragons).

Meld TypeSimples (2–8)Terminals & Honors
Sequence00
Open triplet24
Closed triplet48
Open kan (quad)816
Closed kan (quad)1632

④ Pair

Pair TypeFu
Dragons, seat wind, or round wind2
Anything else0

⑤ Waiting Shape

Wait TypeFuExample
Two-sided wait (ryanmen)0Holding 4-5, waiting on 3 or 6
Edge wait (penchan)2Holding 1-2, waiting on 3
Closed wait (kanchan)2Holding 4-6, waiting on 5
Single tile wait (tanki)2Waiting on the pair tile
Pair wait (shanpon)0Two incomplete triplets, waiting on either

Putting It Together

① Base (20) + ② Winning Method + ③ Melds + ④ Pair + ⑤ Wait = Total Fu

Round the total up to the nearest 10. For example, if your fu add up to 32, your hand is scored as 40 fu.

Exceptions

SituationFu
Pinfu + tsumo — closed hand, all sequences, non-value pair, two-sided wait, won by self-drawFixed 20 fu

Open hand with no fu (sometimes called kuipinfu) — an open hand that would otherwise be 20 fu (e.g. all sequences, ron)Counted as 30 fu

Limit Hands

When a hand reaches 5 or more han, fu no longer matter. The score jumps to a fixed amount based on the han count:
